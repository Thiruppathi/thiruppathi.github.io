100 Days of Reading

It all started with #24in48Readathon. Though I couldn't do full 24 Hours of Reading, I was happy with the outcome. I stared at the result (3 books in 15 Hours); the feeling of affinity swept over me and made a commitment: to read everyday, without missing a day for 100 Days; ergo this shameless brag.

Rules

  • Read book atleast 20 minutes a day
  • Track the progress through Bookly & Goodreads
  • Read more on weekends
  • Don't tweet daily about #100DaysOfReading
  • Don't allow other commitments affect your reading streak
  • 100DaysOfReading shouldn't affect other learning activities(Certifications/MOOCs/R&D)

Lessons Learnt

  • It is ok to read JUST 20 minutes
  • Consistency matters
  • Pomodoro technique helped when not in a mood to read
  • Pause & reflect.
  • Kindle notes & highlights are handy
  • Telling friends & guests that I am on a reading streak helped;
  • Seeing the numbers & statistics of the reading streak was a huge motiviation.
  • Need a better app to track the streak; Bookly tracks only books. I need something to track reading (Tech Blogs/Short Stories etc.,)
  • Paperbook format is not good for Technical Books
  • Don't hesitate to re-read same pages; better understanding and retaining what you read is more important than maintaining a streak
  • Discuss on what you read with friends/like minded people
  • It is alright to put a book on hold if not in a mood
  • Need of the moment decides what book has to be read
  • Binge-Reading is lot better than Binge-Watching;
  • Already exceeded 2018's Reading Goal (Read 23 of 20 Books)
  • Found Optimal hours for Reading
    • One hour before & after bed
    • Commute time to office (1 Hour)
    • Weekend brunch time at neighbourhood cafe
  • It doesn't have to be just a 100 Days Streak; It's difficult to stop the routine.
  • On Day 101, It seemed weird to say, "Let me take a break today"
  • & Ofcourse the lessons from the books
